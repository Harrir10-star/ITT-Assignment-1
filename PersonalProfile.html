What do the results of these tests mean to you?

The results are not at all a surprise to me. I’m naturally an introvert, however like to let me hair down on occasion. I’m likely to sit back and observe what is being said and work through logical analysis before offering up an opinion or view. I prefer to have a plan, I often right lists and tick these off as things are achieved. I like to make sure that I know what the goal is and work towards it. It is very rare that I would not submit something on time.

My learning style is through doing and the pragmatist style therefor sits well with me. I prefer to practise doing something, get hands on. I also like to be given a problem to solve – often this will mean that I will take a little time at the start to think through logical steps before commencing however am focused on the goal.

II like this quote in relation to a pragmatist – well everything accept the last line – I thing that you can be pragmatic staying within the rules, the quote around cutting corners may also mean that the processes or procedures are not always written in a way the is efficient or may just get in the way of doing the right thing.:

A pragmatist is someone who is pragmatic, that is to say, someone who is practical and focused on reaching a goal. A pragmatist usually has a straightforward, matter-of-fact approach and doesn’t let emotion distract her. A pragmatist can also ignore her own ideals to get the job done, so in this way it can have a slightly negative meaning. The word is often used in reference to politicians to mean that they are either sensible or willing to cut corners for their cause.”

How do you think these Results may influence your behaviour in a team?

TThe results wont necessarily influence my behaviour in a team. Over the years I’ve been able work around the weaknesses, sometimes it’s not as efficient but I’ve employed strategies that work. e.g. taking time to work through a situation, going away and thinking through before providing input, using the loudest, or most influential to get a point across. I may get frustrated when there is a lot of talk and not much action, this is when I’m likely to speak up. I like to come away from meetings with clear actions – this includes making sure that actions are sent out afterwards to ensure someone is accountable.

One of the things I need to overcome to make this successful team is the ability to convey my thoughts and experiences. I’m very comfortable with what I’ve achieved from a work perspective, the experiences and opportunities I’ve had however this is mainly from one employer. I can’t draw a lot of examples from comparing one organisation to another.
